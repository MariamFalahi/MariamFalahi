Machine Learning for Early Detection

Breast cancer stands out as the prevalent form of cancer among women globally, making up a quarter of all reported cancer cases. In 2015, it impacted over 2.1 million individuals. The disease initiates when cells in the breast undergo uncontrolled growth, often resulting in the formation of tumours detectable through X-rays or palpable as lumps in the breast region. The primary hurdle in combating breast cancer lies in effectively categorizing tumours as either malignant (cancerous) or benign (non-cancerous).

This Python-based project involves training, testing, and evaluating multiple Machine Learning Classification models including Logistic Regression, K-Nearest Neighbours, Support Vector Machine, Decision Tree, and Random Forest. The objective is to identify the most accurate model for classifying breast cancer tumours as benign or malignant based on their characteristics. Using the optimal values of the model hyperparameters obtained by grid search, the accuracy of the models was evaluated using a confusion matrix along with the mean and standard deviation of the k-fold accuracies, resulting in choosing the Logistic Regression model with 96% accuracy.

Retaining Customers with Deep Learning

In banking, keeping customers is crucial. It ensures steady revenue, lowers acquisition costs, and boosts brand loyalty. Satisfied customers also drive referrals, attracting new business. Prioritizing retention is key for stability, growth, and reputation.

This code employs deep learning with Artificial Neural Networks (ANN) using the TensorFlow library to forecast the likelihood of a customer leaving the bank, considering diverse banking attributes and customer interactions. These features offer insights into customer engagement levels and churn tendencies. The outcomes of the ANN model developed herein can inform proactive measures to retain at-risk customers. The accuracy of the ANN model reached 86%.
